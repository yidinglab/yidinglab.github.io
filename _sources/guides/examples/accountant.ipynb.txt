    "The above approach can only take information before or after, but not during, an iteration.\n",
    "\n",
    "The `Accountant` module provides a way to collect information during an iteration. In short, an `Accountant` can be registered with a `Algorithm`; after that, the accountant is able to collect information from the `Algorithm` when an event fires.\n",
    "\n",
    "The `Algorithm` should report when it fires an event and what event is fired. Alternatively, the source code gives the most accurate information. Consider the `step` method of `SimpleLinearAlgorithm`:\n",
    "\n",
    "``` python\n",
    "class SimpleLinearAlgorithm(Algorithm):\n",
    "    ...\n",
    "\n",
    "    @override\n",
    "    def step(self) -> None:\n",
    "        self.update(\"GENERATION_BEGIN\")\n",
    "\n",
    "        self.population = self.variator.vary_population(self.population)\n",
    "        self.update(\"POST_VARIATION\")\n",
    "\n",
    "\n",
    "        self.evaluator.evaluate_population(self.population)\n",
    "        self.update(\"POST_EVALUATION\")\n",
    "        \n",
    "        self.population = \\\n",
    "            self.selector.select_to_population(self.population)\n",
    "        self.update(\"POST_SELECTION\")\n",
    "```"
